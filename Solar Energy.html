Solar Energy

Solar energy is the Sun's power or energy captured and used to power houses, businesses, cars, trains, etc.
Our sun is basically a large, natural nuclear reactor in space.
The sun releases photons that travel about 93 million miles from the sun to Earth in about 8.5 minutes.
Every hour, there are enough photons that impact our planet and generate enough solar energy to satisfy global needs for an entire year.

When the photons hit a solar cell, they knock electrons loose from their atoms.
If conductors are attached to the positive and negative sides of a cell, it forms an electrical circuit.
When electrons flow through such a circuit, they generate electricity. Multiple cells make up a solar panel, and multiple panels (modules) can be wired together to form a solar array.
The more panels you can deploy, the more energy you can expect to generate.
